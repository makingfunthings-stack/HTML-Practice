World's Best Biryani

Mutton biryani is a fragrant South Asian rice dish made by layering spiced, slow-cooked mutton with long-grain basmati rice, fresh herbs, and whole spices. Cooked on low heat so the flavors meld together, it’s rich, aromatic, and often served with raita or salad.

Ingredients

For mutton and marinade

  • 1 kg mutton (bone-in)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger–garlic paste
  • 2–3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp red chili powder (to taste)
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Handful mint + coriander, chopped

For rice

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamom, 4 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon
  • Salt

For layering

  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced & fried till golden
  • ½ cup warm milk + pinch saffron (or 1 tsp rose water)
  • Ghee or oil

Instructions

  1. Marinate mutton with all marinade ingredients. Rest 1–2 hours (longer = better).
  2. Cook mutton in a heavy pot with 2–3 tbsp oil/ghee. Cover and cook on medium till tender and thick gravy forms.
  3. Parboil rice in plenty of salted water with whole spices till 70% cooked. Drain.
  4. Layer: Over mutton, add half the rice, fried onions, herbs, saffron milk. Repeat with remaining rice.
  5. Dum: Drizzle ghee, cover tightly, cook on low 20–25 min (or 10 min on stove + 10 min resting).
  6. Rest & serve: Gently fluff. Serve hot with raita.
  7. Tip: If mutton is tough, pressure-cook it till just tender before layering.
